What is fake news?

Fake news is false or misleading information presented as news. It is often spread on social media and other online platforms. Fake news can be created for a number of reasons, including to damage the reputation of a person or entity, to promote a political agenda, or to make money through advertising revenue.

How to spot fake news

There are a number of things you can do to spot fake news, including:

  • Check the source. Is the article published on a reputable news website?
  • Consider the author. Are they a credible journalist?
  • Look for evidence. Does the article cite sources? Is the evidence credible?
  • Be wary of sensational headlines. Fake news articles often have headlines that are designed to grab your attention, but they may not be accurate.
  • Read the whole story. Don't just share an article based on the headline. Read the whole story to get the full picture.
  • Check other sources. If you're not sure whether an article is fake, see if other news sources are reporting on the same story.

How to stop the spread of fake news

There are a number of things you can do to stop the spread of fake news, including:

  • Don't share articles that you're not sure are true.
  • Report fake news articles to the websites or platforms where you see them.
  • Educate yourself about fake news and how to spot it.
  • Support reputable news organizations.
